## App Description
*(4000 characters max)*

---

Most planning tools treat your life like a project. Tasks, deadlines, streaks. You ship the week and move on.

Personal Progress is different.

It starts with a question: what kind of person do you want to be this year? From that answer, everything else follows — the areas of life you're tending, the identity you're growing into, the weekly practice of honest reflection that keeps you honest with yourself.

**How it works**

At the start of each year, you write an Annual Letter. Not a goal list — a letter to yourself. A vision. A theme. Something you can return to in March when the momentum fades and in October when you need to remember why any of this matters.

You define your Domains — the areas of life that deserve your attention. Family. Health. Work. Relationships. Creative practice. Whatever is true for you. For each domain, you write an identity statement: not "I want to be healthier" but "I am someone who moves their body every day."

Every week, you sit down for a few minutes of honest reflection. How did the week go? What stood out? What are your top three priorities for the week ahead? It takes less time than you think. Over months, the practice compounds.

Every quarter, you review. You look back at the quarter — what you accomplished, what you'd do differently — and you re-read your annual letter to reconnect with your original vision. Then you adjust.

That's the whole system. Annual vision. Weekly practice. Quarterly review. A closed loop that keeps your daily life in conversation with your yearly purpose.

**What it is not**

Personal Progress does not track habits with streaks. It does not gamify your attention. It has no social features, no sharing, no leaderboard. There is no algorithm deciding what matters to you.

Your data lives on your device. It is never sent anywhere. There are no accounts, no subscriptions hidden behind a paywall, no servers holding your most private thoughts.

This is a tool for serious, private reflection. If you are looking for a dopamine loop, this is the wrong app. If you are looking for a quiet, well-designed space to think clearly about your life, it was built for you.

**Features**

- Annual Letter — write your yearly vision with a word, a theme statement, and a full letter
- Domains — define the areas of your life and the identity you're building in each
- Intentions — set specific intentions per domain, per year
- Weekly Reflection — look back, capture your highlight, set priorities for the week ahead
- Quarterly Reviews — structured review tied to your annual vision
- Year at a Glance — see which domains are on track, at risk, or need attention
- Accountability — subtle status indicators that surface neglected areas without shame
- Weekly reminders — optional Sunday evening notification to sit down and reflect
- Privacy lock — optional Face ID / Touch ID to protect your entries
- Export — take your data with you in plain text at any time
- Fully offline — no internet connection required, ever

**Private by design**

Personal Progress collects no data. There are no analytics, no crash reporting linked to your identity, no advertising. Your reflections, your intentions, your annual letters — they belong to you, stored only on your device.

## Brand Voice Guide

### The person we're writing for
They are thoughtful, not anxious. Ambitious, but not in a way that requires a leaderboard. They've tried the other apps. They found them exhausting or hollow. They want a tool that takes their interior life seriously — that treats them as a whole person, not a productivity unit.

They are probably in their late 20s to 40s. They have enough going on that they need structure. They've figured out that working harder without working intentionally is just running faster in the wrong direction.

### What the voice sounds like
- **Confident, not salesy.** We don't oversell. We describe what the app does and trust the reader to decide.
- **Reflective, not therapeutic.** We don't talk about "healing" or "journeys." We talk about clarity, honesty, and purpose.
- **Precise, not terse.** We use enough words to say the thing clearly. We don't pad.
- **Warm, not familiar.** We respect the reader's intelligence. We don't over-explain or use exclamation points.
- **Private, not exclusive.** The app is for anyone who wants to live intentionally. We don't gatekeep.

### What we never say
- "crush your goals" / "level up" / "unlock your potential"
- "game-changing" / "revolutionary" / any superlative that requires evidence
- "journey" (implies progress requires suffering)
- "community" (there is no community — this is private by design)
- "accountability partner" (the app is not a coach or a friend)
- "hustle" / "grind" / "winning the day"
- "transform your life" (too large a claim; too empty a phrase)
- "self-care" (adjacent to the wellness industry; wrong positioning)
- Any language implying the app knows better than the user what matters

### What we always say clearly
- The app is private. Say it early, say it often, say it plainly.
- The data lives on the device. This is the differentiator — don't bury it.
- The system is a loop: annual letter → domains → weekly reflection → quarterly review → back to annual letter.
- The app is quiet. No streaks, no gamification, no noise.
- The user defines what success looks like. We provide the structure; they provide the meaning.
